Final draft tips and the top 101
By Brian Doolittle - The Sporting News

Nothing guides one through the perils of a fantasy draft better than wisdom. So it is my goal to bestow some wisdom upon users still frantically preparing for their drafts. Below I am listing my current top 101 players, which should help guide you through at least the early rounds of your draft.

If you want rankings for every single NBA player, simply download our NBA fantasy draft kit, which is 116 pages of useful information. There is no way to list all of its contents here, but it has every insight imaginable.

Also, for owners who already have their teams, I am including an update on injured players and recently traded players to get everyone current on how to handle their early-season rosters. If you are pondering any trades or player movement, use the Top 101 as a reference guide in how the proposed transaction measures up.

This preseason has been much more intriguing than any in recent history with Michael Jordan back in action and looking good, Grant Hill returning, the Lakers in search of a title trilogy, a couple of new Afros, many new uniform designs and a high level of rising anticipation -- not seen since Jordan's final season.

The countdown to the regular season can be counted on a single hand now. I know I am primed to melt into my favorite recliner on opening night, kick back with some cold beverages and unhealthy food, and work my remote control like a true professional.

STATUS REPORT: PLAYERS IN LIMBO

F/C Derrick Coleman, 76ers: Philadelphia is banking that Coleman will be productive again. Many forget he was Charlotte's best player two seasons ago -- but he has given people reason to forget with his poor work ethic. He is not a bad gamble though. Do not start him during the first two weeks, but consider using that last roster spot on him.

F Anthony Mason, Bucks: His limbo has now ended, as he landed in Milwaukee. Though the nightlife might not be suited for Mase's taste, he is in a perfect situation. He does not have to score much, and he can focus on defense, rebounding and playing the post. He is a well-rounded player and is worth a starting spot.

G Jalen Rose, Pacers: Like Damon Stoudamire, Rose has tendinitis in his knee and has not had much of a preseason. He is still a top-25 fantasy player, but be careful with him; do not start him in Week 1 unless he busts out right away.

F Robert Traylor, Hornets: Potential and size have been his best traits thus far in his NBA career. The Hornets will give him minutes because they are not deep in the frontcourt, but he is not worth a roster spot heading into the season unless you have a league with 18 or more teams and need depth.

G Rod Strickland, Heat: Many are not convinced that Anthony Carter is prepared to take over where Tim Hardaway left off. If Carter does struggle, Strickland will get a chance to play big minutes. He has All-Star talent, so keep an eye on him.

F Mike Miller, Magic: His broken foot will keep him out until December. With his role not likely to increase with Grant Hill healthy, he is not worth stashing in the meantime unless you are lacking a 3-point specialist.

F Scottie Pippen, Blazers: Pippen has missed two of Portland's six exhibitions with back spasms. His health is questionable and he does not always play with intensity early in the regular season. I'd bench him until he shows productivity.

G Damon Stoudamire, Trail Blazers: His knee troubles have flared up, though it has not been deemed serious. I'd advise against using him the first few games, though, since he is a streaky player anyway.

C Kevin Willis, Rockets: Think Houston is concerned about relying on Kelvin Cato? Willis will see 20 minutes a game, but is nothing more than a stopgap fantasy center. If Cato struggles, Willis might become valuable.

F George Lynch, Hornets: He is a gritty banger inside, but he cannot shoot and is not a valuable fantasy player no matter where he plays -- unless you are desperate for rebounding.
